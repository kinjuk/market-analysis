03 Flags to Revisit
DDM · Model Excluded TSM's dividend growth rate (~14%) exceeds any defensible required return for the stock, making the Gordon Growth Model mathematically undefined. DDM is excluded from the final average. TSM is a growth compounder, not a yield vehicle — this is expected.
Geopolitical Risk · Taiwan Strait No valuation model captures the binary risk of a Taiwan conflict scenario. This is the single largest unquantifiable risk in the thesis. The market applies a structural discount to TSM for this reason — which partially explains why it trades well below its fundamental fair value.
DCF · FCF Volatility TSM's FCF is highly volatile due to lumpy capex cycles (−44% in 2023, +177% in 2024). The 2026 base of $35B assumes continued AI-driven demand. If capex accelerates faster than revenue, FCF compresses and the DCF output drops materially.
FX Risk · NTD/USD TSM reports in NTD. ADR valuations carry ~100% sensitivity to USD/NTD exchange rate movements. A strengthening USD inflates reported figures; a weakening USD compresses them. All USD figures here assume current exchange rates.
Average · Weighting Four models receive equal weight. Graham and Multiples are bullish; Lynch and DCF are conservative. A DDM-replacement model (e.g. excess return or H-model) would be more appropriate for TSM and should be added in a future revision.
